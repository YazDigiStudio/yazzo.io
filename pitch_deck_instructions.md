# Yazzo Pitch Deck — Update Instructions

These instructions reflect the updated positioning of Yazzo as a city-wide real-time layer,
not just a nightlife and dining app. Update each slide accordingly.

---

## Core Positioning Shift

**Old framing:** Real-time deals for concerts, theatre, and restaurants.
**New framing:** Yazzo is the real-time layer on top of the city — a better local advertising
platform than Facebook, covering everything from dining and music to yoga, dance, fitness,
art, and kids activities.

Two things to remove everywhere:
- "Venues" → use "local businesses and organizers" or "partners"
- "Offers/deals" as the only mechanic → Yazzo also has paid visibility listings (not just deals)

---

## Slide-by-Slide Instructions

### Slide 1 — Cover
- Change "Own the Moment!" to "Own the City" to match the updated brand direction
- "REAL-TIME CAPACITY FOR CITIES." works well — keep it
- Update launch date if it has passed

---

### Slide 2 — The Problem
**Current text is too narrow.** It only mentions concerts, theatre, and restaurants.

Update to reflect the full range of partners:
- Restaurants with slow lunch hours
- Yoga studios and dance schools with open class spots
- Theatres and concert venues with unsold tickets
- Sports facilities with unused court time
- Art galleries with low foot traffic

Also update the core insight: the problem is not just empty seats —
it is that local businesses have no real-time, location-aware way to reach
people nearby. Social media ads are too slow, too broad, and too expensive
for small operators.

Suggested new copy:
> Cities are full of unused capacity every day.
> Yoga classes. Restaurant tables. Dance courses. Concert seats. Art exhibitions.
> They exist. People are nearby. But they don't meet in time.
> Current advertising — social media, flyers, email — is planned in advance.
> Life happens in real time.

### Slide 6 — Business Model
The deck shows one model (SaaS subscription + reservation fee). The new positioning
introduces two clearer tiers. Consider restructuring:

**Tier 1 — Visibility listing (advertising)**
Partner pays a flat monthly fee to appear on the map.
No booking required. Works like a local ad — better targeted than Facebook
because users are actively looking for something to do nearby.

**Tier 2 — Bookable offer (marketplace)**
Partner publishes a time-limited listing. User books and pays in-app.
Yazzo takes a commission per transaction.

This maps to: recurring subscription revenue (Tier 1) + transaction revenue (Tier 2).
Keep "Recurring base + scalable upside" — it still applies perfectly.
